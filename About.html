Objectives

  • To quantitatively measure the in a comprehensive manner
  • To estimate the and hence the fall risk category of a given individual as per the benchmark gait standards , using the measured gait parameters and their medical history
  • To classify the of the individual under consideration with an objective of providing some early indications about a few imminent motor function disabilities

Why GaiT Watch ?

  • Medical practitioners do not have an easy to use technology based intervention to objectively measure the spatio temporal kinematics related to walking
  • They rely on varied methods of subjective assessments to assess the fall risk of their patients – the widely used Morse fall risk assessment model does not incorporate objective measurements of gait biomechanics.
  • The methodology currently available is to use sophisticated Gait Labs which are too costly to use and are available in selected facilities only.
  • Gait labs are not affordable for all especially in the Indian context.
  • No screening devices available currently to catch neuro muscular disorders in early stage.
  • No mechanisms are currently available to chronologically track the disease progression / efficacy of therapeutic intervention in a systematic way.
  • Use of Technology interventions in the alternative medical practices like Ayurvedic and Siddha is very less

Salient Features

User-friendly

The standalone user friendly wearable device is primarily meant for predicting and the fall risk of geriatric population.

Assist Medical Practitioners

Assists a medical practitioner or physiotherapist by measuring the gait metrics like step length, stride length, velocity, cadence, swing phase and stance phase to comprehensively assess and quantify the gait wellness of an individual.

Risk Prediction

Notify the predicted risk categorization to the caregivers.

Regular Gait Monitoring

Supports the geriatric population with Parkinson's disease and similar disorders by monitoring at regular intervals and evaluate the effects of therapeutic interventions / disease progression

Rehabilitation Support

Support the individuals in rehabilitation stage after spine related injuries, stroke survivors etc. by monitoring their gait posture recovery.

Fall Risk Prediction

Early warning of fall risk helps in initiation of appropriate safety measures and avoidance of the huge health care cost associated with geriatric falls

Beneficiaries

Neuromuscular Degenerative disorders

Patients with Neurological disorders, Dementia, Parkinson’s , Diabetic Neuropathy, and Stroke

Motor Impairments

Patients with Motor Function Disabilities such as Spinal cord injury, Cerebral palsy and Arthritis

Sports / other injuries

People under rehabilitation after sports or other injuries. Also People under post operative care.

Senior Citizens

Senior Citizens with or without gait impairments

How to use this device

  • Initial interview of the patient - 10 minutes

  • Record the patient details through the associated mobile app

  • Switch on the device and fasten it firmly just below the knee of the person to be tested

  • Ask the person to walk for 30 seconds to 1 minute / 20 meters in their naturally possible gait. At the end of the walking session, the gait report of the wearer is automatically generated

  • The Gait metrics and the Gait report can be readily viewed on the screen of the tablet and also can be downloaded and printed.

Gait Watch – Specifications

  • The fall risk category of the individual as well as the fall risk score calculated as per the standard Morse Fall Risk Model . The comprehensive report encompasses the following gait metrics of the individual tested: step length stride length velocity cadence Range of Motion
  • Class A device with less than minimum risk involved

  • Operating maximum voltage 3.7V and maximum current 2A

  • Ethical Clearance for Human Trails by Institutional Human Ethical Clearance Committee (IHEC) in January 2021

  • Cleared all the wearable device testing standards : EMI/EMC Testing, Specific Absorption Rate (SAR), biocompatibility
