Effects and Causes of Geriatric Falls

  • The morbidity due to falls are serious injuries, fractures, loss of independence, functional decline and permanent disability
  • The health care cost associated with falls is substantial
  • Several intrinsic and extrinsic factors cause falls in elderly people
  • The common risk factors for falls are depression, impairments in gait, balance, cognition, vision, activities of daily living
  • Chronic conditions like arthritis, stroke, incontinence, diabetes, Parkinson’s, Dementia also cause fall

Gait Complications

The morbidity due to falls includes serious injuries, fractures and permanent disability. Risk factors for falls include muscle weakness, history of falls, impairments in gait, cognition, vision and chronic conditions including Arthritis, Stroke, Parkinson’s disease, and Dementia.


The Need for Fall Risk Prediction


Fall is considered as one of the most serious problems for the elderly. Preventing falls remains one of the highest health care priorities and hence prediction of fall risk category has started gaining prominence in geriatric health care facilities.

Smart Wearable Devices in Modern Health Care System

Wearable devices can provide patient monitoring, surveillance, screening, diagnosis, and assistance with treatment, post-treatment, and ongoing management. These devices also determine, and confirm efficiency of treatment based on real-time physiological feedback.
